# Singapore Banking Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| MAS | Monetary Authority of Singapore — the central bank / regulator |
| MAS 610 | Monthly statistical return on bank assets & liabilities |
| NPL | Non-Performing Loan — >90 DPD or classified impaired |
| NII | Net Interest Income = interest earned − interest paid |
| NIM | Net Interest Margin = annualised NII ÷ average earning assets |
| ECL | Expected Credit Loss — IFRS 9 forward-looking provisioning model |
| LTV | Loan-to-Value = outstanding loan ÷ property value |
| TDSR | Total Debt Servicing Ratio — MAS caps this at 55% of gross income |
| SDIC | Deposit insurance — covers up to SGD 100,000 per depositor per bank |
| PDPA | Personal Data Protection Act — governs customer data handling |
| SORA | Singapore Overnight Rate Average — benchmark rate for SGD loans |
| DPD | Days Past Due |
| ALCO | Asset & Liability Committee — manages interest rate / funding risk |

---

### Why this matters for interviews

Singapore bank interviewers (DBS, OCBC, UOB, StanChart) often ask candidates to explain
*what* a metric means before asking them to write the SQL for it. Knowing that NPL = >90 DPD,
or that TDSR is capped at 55%, signals you understand the business — not just the syntax.
